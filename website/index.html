I am a fifth year Ph.D student from George Tseng's group in Department of Biostatistics from University of Pittsburgh, under the supervision of George Tseng and Yong Seok Park. Prior to that, I received my master's degree in Physics in University of Pittsburgh in 2012 and my bachlor's degree in Harbin Institute of Technology in 2011.

My research interest lies in both statistical methodology and application on genomics and bioinformatics. I am particularly interested in genomic data integration, modeling and variable selection in high-dimensional data, graphical model, Bayesian methods, optimization and statistical computing. I have collaborated with biologists in the fields of cancer and psychiatry to analysis a broad range of genomic data, which motivates me to develop practical methodology and user-friendly software.
